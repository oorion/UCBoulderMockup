Understanding Our Fiscal Code of Ethics Policy

All employees of the University of Colorado contribute to the quality of our institution, from the decisions made by the President, to every interaction each of us has with students, and everything in between. We all work together to develop an environment of imagination, creativity and discovery. In order to continue growing as a research institution, it is imperative that we manage our finances in a way that allows others to place their trust in us. As a result, it is the responsibility of all employees to work together to promote fiscally responsible behavior. To ensure that we continue to operate effectively as an organization, and that all employees understand their role, we've provided a summary below of several key fiscal responsibilities we all share.

Preserving University Resources

Every Employee is responsible for ensuring that University resources are used in a prudent manner and for their designated purposes in accordance with policies, laws, regulations, contracts, grants and donor restrictions. As an employee, it is your responsibility to support your department's operational plans using professional judgment and reasonable care with regard to University resources.

Disclose Any Potential Conflicts of Interest

All employees are required to disclose existing or potential conflicts of interest. Employees who fail to disclose conflicts of interest may be subject to criminal penalties in accordance with state criminal statutes.

Notify the University of Financial Misconduct

All employees are required to notify the University in the event of known or suspected fiscal misconduct, including but not limited to:

  • Embezzlement
  • Unauthorized use of goods, services, or resources
  • Diversion of assets
  • Conflicts of interest that result in financial loss
  • Violation of University fiscal policies and procedures for personal gain
